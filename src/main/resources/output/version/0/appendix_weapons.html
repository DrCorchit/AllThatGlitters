Weapon Modifiers

Certain weapons have special properties, which affect how they behave:

  • Cut-and-Thrust: The weapon can deal either piercing or slashing damage.
  • Nimble: Dexterity can be used to provide the weapon's damage, instead of strength.
  • Improvised: The item is not designed as a weapon. The damage die is rolled with advantage.
  • Light: The weapon can be used alongside a greatshield, or dual wielded alongside another light weapon.
  • Two-Handed: The weapon must be handled with two hands when attacking.
  • Sharp: The critical threshold for the weapon is reduced by 1. This property is temporarily lost after critical failing an attack roll, but can be regained by using a whetstone.
  • Polearm: The weapon can hit targets 10 feet away instead of 5, and is compatible with the Polarm Charge ability.
  • Throwable: The weapon does not gain the Improvised trait when thrown.
  • Crossbow: The weapon is a mechanical crossbow. It requires 30 seconds to reload, and its damage does not benefit from the user's attribute scores.
  • Firearm: The weapon is a firearm and deals force damage. It requires 1 minute to reload, and its damage does not benefit from the user's attribute scores.
  • Parrying: The weapon increases mitigation by the indicated amount when the parry reaction is taken.
  • Blocking: The weapon increases BC by a certain amount, doubled when actively blocking.
  • Mordhau: The weapon can be held by the blade, using the crossguard as a hammer. This changes the weapon’s damage type to bludgeoning, and replaces the damage die. (Larger swords have this property.)
  • Versatile: The weapon can be held in an alternate grip for extra damage.
  • Projectile: The weapon fires a specific type of projectile
  • Ranged: The weapon has a range: X/Y. Attacks against targets beyond X feet away have disadvantage; attacks against targets beyond Y feet away automatically fail.

Small Weapons

Small weapons are those which are easily wielded in one hand, including everyday tools improvised as weapons. They require no especial skill to use, are easily concealed, and are typically wielded by civilians. These weapons automatically have the light property, and can be dual-wielded or used in the offhand.

Cleaver 1d6 + STR Slashing 2 Light, Improvised A heavy knife for cutting meat.
Club 1d6 + STR Bludgeoning 1 Light, Improvised Any hefty stick or chair leg will do.
Dagger 1d4 + STR/DEX Cut-and-Thrust 5 Light, Nimble, Sharp, Throwable, Ranged: 20/60 A short sidearm, suitable for slashing or stabbing. The “Sharp” property does not apply when thrown.
Hatchet 1d4 + STR/DEX Slashing 5 Light, Nimble, Throwable, Ranged: 20/60 A small axe suitable for throwing
Knife 1d4 + STR/DEX Piercing 1 Light, Nimble, Improvised An ordinary workman’s knife
Sickle 1d6 + STR/DEX Slashing 5 Light, Nimble, Improvised A farmer’s tool of dubious sharpness. May be modified by a blacksmith to lose the Improvised trait, for 50 gold.
Stiletto 1d4 + STR/DEX Piercing 5 Light, Nimble, Sharp A stabbing knife, long and sharp. Ignores BC from chainmail.
Tanto 1d4 + STR/DEX Slashing 10 Light, Nimble, Sharp A short slashing blade with an angular tip.
