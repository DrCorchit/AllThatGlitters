Any sensible treatise on magic will disclose the following insight: magic is energy. Therefore, magic is classified according to whence this energy originates, the techniques used to manipulate it, and finally according to the overall purpose of its application. In fact, all spells follow the same basic pattern, whereby the magus identifies a source of energy, imposes order over chaos, and finally unleashes this directed energy upon the world.

Despite being intensely studied, magic remains an abstruse and opaque subject. Among those who study it, there is no consensus as to why certain individuals wield its power so effortlessly, why certain spells are so difficult to reproduce, and why other spells work at all. This inconvenience doesn't stop scholars from doing the one thing they are truly good at, however: categorizing things.

A long time ago, spells were categorized into two types: thaumaturgy and divination. Thaumaturgical disciplines used the suffix -urgy, while divination schools used the suffix -mancy. For example, there was once a distinction between necromancy and necromurgy; the former was concerned with contacting deceased spirits, while the latter with raising their bodies. However, centuries of expanding magical theory as well as a good deal of linguistic bastardization have relegated such distinction to a mere footnote of history. Modern magic is divided into six schools of sorcery, which each have their own disciplines. These are listed below:

Alchemy

An ancient school focused on interactions between magic and chemistry. It is said that the secret is in neither the magic nor the mixture, but in the harmony between the two. Alchemical spells are always enchantment rituals, where the magic is cast upon a potion or poison as it is brewed. Most potions lose their magical properties after a long rest, but can be restored via a short ritual. Optimal results are obtained when performed at an alchemical laboratory, but can be performed using field equipment in a pinch.

Primary Attribute: Intelligence

Casting Skill: Literacy

Disciplines

  • Pharmacology: The study of beneficial brews and potions.
  • Toxicology: The study of toxins and potions with detrimental effects.
Astrology

The magic of the spheres and the vault of the heavens. Spells of this category vary significantly, allowing one to deal damage, heal, invoke insanity, and travel instantaneously through the cosmos.

Primary Attribute: Intelligence

Casting Skill: Literacy

Disciplines

  • Solar Astrology: The subdivision of astrology concerned with the sun and stars. Focuses on radiance and healing.
  • Lunar Astrology: The subdivision of astrology connected with the moon and its phases. Focuses on darkness and insanity.
  • Planetary Astrology: The subdivision of astrology focused on the movement and alignment of the lesser spheres. Focuses on transportaion and teleportation.
Biomancy

The magic of living things, of the wild, and the human body. Magical techniques of this school are explored chiefly by the druids, who detest the term "biomancy" and have other names for their craft, such as wyrdworking and shamanism. Spells of this school are often not taught at a university level, and must be obtained from druid circles or the mad whisperings of the fey themselves.

Primary Attribute: Charisma

Casting Skill: Nature

Disciplines

  • Weal: The magic of plants, often used for healing or poisoning.
  • Wild: The magic of animals, often used for transformations.
  • Wyrd: The magic of the fey. Spells of this discipline are as chaotic and unpredictable as the fey themselves.
Conjuration

Primary Attribute: Charisma

Casting Skill: Devotion

Disciplines

  • Divination: The practice of summoning spirits for communication. By working this school the magus may acquire a great deal of knowledge concerning the future, the past, and faroff lands.
  • Invocation: The practice of summoning spirits in order to produce physical manifestations.
  • Necromancy: An amalgamation of iniquitous practices which seek to maim, desecrate, and spew disease, as well as contacting and raising the dead. The entire discipline is utterly forbidden.
Elementurgy

Primary Attribute: Willpower

Casting Skill: Sorcery

Disciplines

  • Aerokinesis: Elemental air magic. Focuses on forces such as wind, clouds, and lightning .
  • Geokinesis: Elemental earth magic. Focuses on substances such as clay, stone, and metal.
  • Hydrokinesis: Elemental water magic. Focuses on substances such as water, snow, and ice.
  • Pyrokinesis: Elemental fire magic. Focuses on the forces of heat, flame, and lava.
Psionics

Primary Attribute: Willpower

Casting Skill: Sorcery

Disciplines

  • Illusion: Magic concerned with conveying false sensory impressions and misdirection, and dispelling the same.
  • Telekinesis: Magic concerned with the movement of objects by mental impulse alone.
  • Telepathy: Explores magically aided communication, and various forms of mind control and mental domination.
