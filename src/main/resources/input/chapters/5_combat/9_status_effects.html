Sometimes characters fall prey to certain temporary conditions that reduce their effectiveness in combat. These conditions are called status effects and begin and end under certain conditions. Also, some status effects can accumulate, meaning they can be inflicted multiple times on the same character.

Name Effect Typical Causes Recovery Strategy Notes
Bleeding Accumulates. Each turn, the target takes damage equal to the number of turns of bleeding remaining, and then lowers their bleeding level by 1. Inflicted by slashing damage. Alleviated by a short rest, or when an ally takes the "help" action and passes a medicine check. Enemies not composed of flesh are immune.
Blinded The target cannot see, has disadvantage on attack rolls, and automatically fails any ability checks and saving throws based on sight. Poison or intense light. Antidote, or wait until vision returns naturally. Some characters that use echolocation or magical sight are immune to this condition.
Burning The target takes 1d4 fire damage each turn. If accumulated burning exceeds the target's WILL score at the start of the target's turn, they are incapacitated by pain for that turn. Fire and radiant damage. Alleviated by water and the Fire Mitigation Technique ability.
Deafened The target cannot hear, and automatically fails ability checks and saving throws based on hearing. Loud sounds and explosions. Wait until hearing returns naturally. If the target navigates via echolocation, it has disability on attack rolls.
Incapacitated The target cannot move or take actions, and automatically fails ability checks and saving throws. DC is reduced to zero. A variety of causes. Wait a set number of turns, or a set number of hours (usually 1d4). Several status effects inflict this condition.
Intoxicated Accumulates. Reduces DEX and INT by 1 for every level. The target becomes incapacitated and falls unconscious for 1d4 hours when intoxication level exceeds strength score. Inflicted by alcohol and certain drugs or poisons. One level of intoxication is lost per hour.
Freezing Accumulates. The target's DEX is reduced by 1 for each turn of accumulated freezing. Freezing decreases by 1 at the end of every turn, or when cancelled out by fire damage. Ice damage and spells. Fire damage, or short rest. Progresses to frozen when total freezing exceeds the target's STR score.
Frozen The target is encased in ice and incapacitated. They vulnerable to force and bludgeoning, but resistant to all other forms of damage. Freezing buildup. Short rest. Ends burning and pauses poisoned and bleeding status effects.
Petrified The target is encased in stone, incapacitated. Petrification spells Basilisk Oil. Extremely rare.
Poisoned After a set number of turns, the poison takes effect. The effect depends on the specific spell Poisons and certain spells. Alleviated by long resting or the antidote consumable.
Prone The target cannot move and DC is halved, but ranged attacks have disadvantage. Force damage and various abilities. Use a main hand or offhand action to stand up.

Note: the freezing and burning status effects are mutually exclusive. One turn of freezing cancels out one turn of burning, and vice versa.
