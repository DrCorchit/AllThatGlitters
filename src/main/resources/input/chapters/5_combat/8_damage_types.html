Common Damage Types:
  • Slashing: Liable to cause bleeding.
  • Bludgeoning: +3 to hit versus plate armors.
  • Piercing: +3 to hit versus mail armors.
  • Fire: Liable to cause burning.
  • Poisoning: Deals additional effects, depending on the type of poison.
  • Force: Dealt from explosions. Liable to knock the target prone.
Rare Damage Types:
  • Ice: Liable to cause freezing.
  • Lightning: Voids BC from metal armor.
  • Corrosive: Causes armor damage which must later be repaired at a smithy.
  • Holy: An extraordinarily bright light causes extra damage to undead and evil aligned creatures.
  • Profane: A sickening miasma of spiritual darkness causes extra damage to good-aligned creatures.

A few notes on damage types:

  • Slashing, fire, and ice damage deal one level of their respective status effects per hit.
  • When a character takes force damage exceeding their strength score, they must make a strength saving throw with DT equal to the force damage dealt or be knocked prone.
  • Successful attacks with acid decrease the target's BC by 1, until zero. The armor can be repaired at a blacksmith for 20 gold per BC lost.
  • Good-aligned characters possess resistance to holy damage, and vulnerability to profane damage. Conversely, evil-aligned characters and the undead possess resistance to profane damage and vulnerability to holy damage.
Vulnerability, Resistance, and Immunity

Vulnerability, resistance, and immunity affect the damage from specific damage types, as follows:

  • Vulnerability: Damage is doubled.
  • Resistance: Damage is halved.
  • Immunity: Damage is voided.
